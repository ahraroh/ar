Ahra Roh, OTR/L, ATP

Meet Ahra

Ahra Roh, OTR/L and Assistive Technology Practitioner, is a board-certified Occupational Therapist. She has been actively practicing occupational therapy since 2012, and her primary practice areas include adult and geriatric physical rehabilitation.

Education

  • University of Southern California
    • Master of Arts, Occupational Therapy and Occupational Science May 2012
    • Bachelor of Arts, Neuroscience May 2010

Experties

Ahra has substantial experience in various rehabilitation settings, including Intensive Care Units, acute hospitals, and acute rehabilitation facilities. Currently, she is a Senior OT at the California Rehabilitation Institute – a joint venture of UCLA and Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. Located in the Century City neighborhood of Los Angeles, CRI is the largest physical medicine and rehabilitation hospital of its kind on the West Coast.

Ahra is officially certified as a Kinesiotaping Practitioner, Assistive Technolgy Practitioner, and in Physical Agent Modalities (PAM) for the use of various electrical stimulation and ultrasound devices. She has extensive training and experience with manual therapies, such as Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation (PNF) and other neuro re-education treatments. She also completed the Assistive Technology Application certificate program at California State University, Northridge in 2018.

Background

Ahra possesses a diverse cultural background. She spent her early years in South Korea before moving to Indonesia for middle school and high school. Ahra set roots in Los Angeles in 2006 and attended college and graduate school at the University of Southern California, where she earned both her B.A. in Neuroscience and M.A. in Occupational Therapy and Occupational Science.

Vision

Ahra strongly embraces the philosophy that – even after illness or injury – every individual is capable of achieving a meaningful and fulfilling quality of life through successful rehabilitation. She has helped and witnessed many patients experience remarkable recoveries under the skilled therapy provided by herself and her colleagues.

Ahra is now committed to expanding her private practice to deliver this same expertise to patients who urgently seek further intervention after being discharged from traditional rehabilitation settings.

Personal

In her personal time, Ahra enjoys watercolor painting and digital art. She also enjoys healthy discussions about finances and the world economy with friends over the dinner table.
